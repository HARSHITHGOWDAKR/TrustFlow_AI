TRUSTFLOW COMPLIANCE POLICY AND PROCEDURES
Version 1.0 - Effective April 1, 2026

1. DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY POLICY

1.1 Overview
TrustFlow is committed to protecting the privacy and security of all personal data and sensitive information collected, processed, and stored within our systems. We comply with all applicable data protection regulations including GDPR, CCPA, and SOC2 Type II requirements.

1.2 Data Collection and Processing
- All data collection is performed with explicit user consent
- Personal data is collected only for specified, explicit purposes
- We maintain detailed records of processing activities
- Data minimization principles guide all collection efforts
- Processing is limited to lawful bases: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interests

1.3 Data Retention
- Personal data is retained only as long as necessary
- Maximum retention period is 7 years for compliance records
- Deletion requests are processed within 30 days
- Secure destruction methods ensure data cannot be recovered
- Retention schedules are reviewed and updated annually

1.4 Security Measures
- Encryption at rest using AES-256
- Encryption in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Multi-factor authentication required for all system access
- Role-based access control limits data visibility
- Activity logging captures all access to sensitive data
- Annual security audits performed by third-party assessors

2. ACCESS CONTROL AND AUTHENTICATION

2.1 User Access Management
- All users must complete annual security training
- Access is granted on a need-to-know basis
- Managers approve access requests with business justification
- Access provisioning takes no more than 5 business days
- Dormant accounts are disabled after 90 days of inactivity

2.2 Multi-Factor Authentication
- All administrative access requires MFA
- MFA devices must be time-synchronized authenticators
- Backup codes are generated for account recovery
- MFA is encrypted and stored securely

2.3 Password Management
- Passwords must be at least 12 characters
- Password complexity requirements enforced by system
- Passwords changed every 90 days
- Previous 12 passwords cannot be reused
- Password resets completed within 24 hours

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE AND BREACH NOTIFICATION

3.1 Incident Classification
- Level 1: No data exposure, isolated to one system
- Level 2: Limited data exposure affecting <10 records
- Level 3: Significant data exposure affecting 10-1000 records
- Level 4: Critical breach affecting >1000 records

3.2 Breach Notification
- Regulatory breaches notified within 72 hours
- Affected individuals notified without undue delay
- Notification includes breach nature, data involved, recommendations
- Documentation maintained for regulatory review
- Root cause analysis completed within 2 weeks

3.3 Response Time SLA
- Level 1 incidents: 24 hour response time
- Level 2 incidents: 12 hour response time
- Level 3 incidents: 4 hour response time
- Level 4 incidents: 1 hour response time

4. AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

4.1 Internal Audits
- Quarterly security assessments
- Annual penetration testing by third parties
- Vulnerability scanning performed weekly
- Audit logs retained for minimum 2 years

4.2 External Audits
- SOC2 Type II audit performed annually
- GDPR compliance assessment annually
- Results documented and stored securely
- Remediation plans for identified issues tracked to completion

4.3 Third-Party Risk Management
- All vendors assessed for security compliance
- Vendor agreements include security and privacy provisions
- Annual vendor security assessments required
- Sub-processor list maintained and updated

5. EMPLOYEE TRAINING AND AWARENESS

5.1 Security Training Requirements
- New employees complete training within 30 days
- Annual refresher training mandatory
- Role-specific training for sensitive positions
- Training completion records maintained
- Phishing simulations conducted quarterly

5.2 Acceptable Use Policy
- Personal use of systems limited to incidental use
- Prohibited: Unauthorized access, data theft, malware distribution
- Monitoring: System administrators may audit activity
- Violations: Disciplinary action up to termination

6. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

6.1 Backup Strategy
- Daily incremental backups
- Weekly full backups
- Backups stored in geographically diverse locations
- Recovery testing quarterly
- Recovery time objective: 4 hours
- Recovery point objective: 1 hour of data loss maximum

6.2 Disaster Recovery Plan
- Plan documented and tested annually
- Failover procedures documented
- Critical systems identified and prioritized
- Alternative processing sites identified
- Key personnel trained on procedures

7. POLICY REVIEW AND UPDATES

This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed to reflect:
- Changes in regulatory requirements
- Evolution of security threats
- Organization restructuring
- Lessons learned from incidents
- Industry best practices

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ABOUT COMPLIANCE

Q: What is our data retention policy?
A: Personal data is retained only as long as necessary, with a maximum retention period of 7 years for compliance records. Deletion requests are processed within 30 days using secure destruction methods.

Q: How do we handle data breaches?
A: We classify breaches by severity and notify regulatory authorities within 72 hours if required. Affected individuals are notified immediately with details of the breach and recommended actions.

Q: What security controls protect our data?
A: We use AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.2+ encryption in transit, MFA for all administrative access, RBAC for data access control, and comprehensive activity logging.

Q: How often is security audited?
A: We perform quarterly security assessments, annual penetration testing, weekly vulnerability scanning, and annual SOC2 Type II audits by external parties.

Q: What training is required?
A: All employees complete security training within 30 days of hire, with annual refresher training mandatory. Role-specific training provided for positions with access to sensitive data.

Q: What should I do if I suspect a security incident?
A: Contact the Security Operations Center immediately at security@trustflow.com or call the incident hotline. Provide as much detail as possible about the suspected incident.
